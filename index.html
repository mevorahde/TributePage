Jerry Rice

Jerry Rice scoring a TD against the Vikings in 1993.

Here's a time line of Jerry Rice's life and career:

  • 1962 - Oct 13, born in Starkville, Mississippi
  • 1981 - Started his college career at Mississippi Valley State University
  • 1985 - Drafted in the first round of the NFL Draft by the San Francisco 49ers
  • 1989 - Wins his first Super Bowl championship with a win against the Cincinnati Bengals and is named Super Bowl MVP of Super Bowl XXIII
  • 1990 - Wins his second Super Bowl championship with a win against the Denver Broncos in Super Bowl XXIV
  • 1995 - Wins his third Super Bowl championship with a win against the San Diego Chargers in Super Bowl XXIX
  • 1997 - Rice tears his anterior cruciate and medial collateral ligaments in his left knee in the first game of the regular season, forcing him to miss most of the season
  • 2001 - After being released by the 49ers, Rice signs with the Oakland Raiders
  • 2003 - Rice helps the Raiders to reach Super Bowl Super Bowl XXXVII against the Tampa Bay Buccaneers
  • 2004 - After requesting to be moved, Rice is traded midseason to the Seattle Seahawks. He plays his last professional NFL game in a wildcard loss to the St. Louis Rams
  • 2006 - The 49ers announced that Rice would sign a contract with them, allowing him to retire as a member of the team.
  • 2010 - Jerry Rice is inducted into the Pro Football Hall of Fame
